I'm a UX/UI designer and product manager who turns ambiguous problems into clear, shippable outcomes — one usable screen, scoped PRD, and iterated release at a time.

My favorite kind of work lives at the seam between research and shipping: customer interviews on Monday, a clickable prototype by Wednesday, a usability pass on Friday, a release the following sprint. I keep a tight loop between what I'm hearing from users and what's actually being built.

Across Mirabend, EasyClimbTech, and DayOneLead, I've shipped 0→1 product surfaces in domains as different as game-engine asset pipelines, AI mock interviews, and B2B sales feeds. The thread that runs through all of it: clear navigation, honest empty/error states, and a relentless edit on every screen until nothing on it is decorative.

Outside of design tools I'm comfortable enough in JavaScript, React, and Python to read engineering's PRs, prototype in code when Figma isn't enough, and write the spec in language the team can actually build from.
